# Russian Propaganda Pattern Taxonomy

This document defines the structured taxonomy of Russian propaganda tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) used for analysis of text content.

## Overview

This taxonomy is grounded in:
- Mitrokhin Archive active measures documentation
- Modern Russian information operations research
- Academic analysis of Russian propaganda patterns
- Documented cases of Russian influence campaigns

## Propaganda Pattern Categories

### 1. Narrative Frameworks

#### ENCIRCLEMENT_NARRATIVE
- **Definition**: Portrayal of Russia as surrounded by hostile Western/NATO powers
- **Key Phrases**: "surrounded", "encircled", "threatened borders", "NATO expansion"
- **Example**: "Russia has no choice but to defend itself against NATO's aggressive expansion eastward."

#### NATO_EXPANSION_EXISTENTIAL_THREAT
- **Definition**: Framing NATO expansion as existential threat requiring defensive response
- **Key Phrases**: "existential threat", "defensive response", "red lines", "security guarantees"
- **Example**: "NATO's eastward expansion represents an existential threat to Russian security."

#### WESTERN_HYPOCRISY_FRAMING
- **Definition**: Highlighting perceived Western double standards and moral failures
- **Key Phrases**: "Western hypocrisy", "double standards", "selective morality"
- **Example**: "The West condemns Russia while ignoring its own violations of international law."

#### BOTH_SIDES_FALSE_EQUIVALENCY
- **Definition**: Creating false moral equivalencies between Russian and Western actions
- **Key Phrases**: "both sides", "equally guilty", "no difference", "same actions"
- **Example**: "Both Russia and NATO have engaged in aggressive expansionism."

### 2. Rhetorical Techniques

#### WHATABOUTISM
- **Definition**: Deflecting criticism by pointing to Western actions
- **Key Phrases**: "What about", "But the West", "America also", "Look at"
- **Example**: "What about America's invasion of Iraq? How is that different?"

#### UKRAINE_CORRUPTION_NARRATIVE
- **Definition**: Emphasizing Ukrainian government corruption to justify actions
- **Key Phrases**: "corrupt Kiev regime", "Nazi government", "illegitimate authorities"
- **Example**: "Russia is fighting against the corrupt Nazi regime in Kiev."

#### SANCTIONS_COUNTER_NARRATIVE
- **Definition**: Portraying sanctions as ineffective or harmful to imposing countries
- **Key Phrases**: "sanctions backfire", "economic weapon", "hurting ordinary people"
- **Example**: "Western sanctions are backfiring and hurting their own economies more than Russia's."

### 3. Cultural/Civilizational Themes

#### US_DECLINE_DEGENERACY
- **Definition**: Narrative of Western/American moral and political decline
- **Key Phrases**: "declining West", "moral degeneracy", "failing democracy", "collapsing empire"
- **Example**: "The declining American empire desperately tries to maintain its hegemony."

#### TRADITIONAL_VALUES_DEFENSE
- **Definition**: Positioning Russia as defender of traditional family/religious values
- **Key Phrases**: "traditional values", "family values", "Christian civilization", "moral decay"
- **Example**: "Russia stands as the last bastion of traditional Christian values against Western moral decay."

#### MULTIPOLAR_WORLD_ORDER
- **Definition**: Promoting vision of multipolar world order challenging US hegemony
- **Key Phrases**: "multipolar world", "end of hegemony", "new world order", "sovereign equality"
- **Example**: "The emerging multipolar world order will replace American hegemony with sovereign equality."

### 4. Geopolitical Framing

#### ILLEGITIMATE_SECURITY_CONCERNS
- **Definition**: Framing aggressive actions as legitimate security responses
- **Key Phrases**: "legitimate security concerns", "defensive measures", "forced response"
- **Example**: "Russia's actions are a forced response to legitimate security concerns."

#### HISTORICAL_GRIEVANCES
- **Definition**: Invoking historical injustices to justify current actions
- **Key Phrases**: "historical injustice", "never again", "lessons of history", "ancestral lands"
- **Example**: "Russia will never again allow the historical injustices suffered in the 1990s."

#### GEOPOLITICAL_NEUTRALITY_FRAMING
- **Definition**: Presenting pro-Russian positions as neutral, balanced analysis
- **Key Phrases**: "neutral perspective", "balanced view", "objective analysis", "both sides"
- **Example**: "Taking a neutral perspective, one can see legitimate concerns on both sides."

## Scoring Dimensions

### RUSSIAN_NARRATIVE_ALIGNMENT (0-5)
Measures how closely content aligns with documented Russian state messaging:
- **0**: No alignment with Russian narratives
- **1**: Minimal alignment, mostly factual content
- **2**: Some alignment with Russian talking points
- **3**: Moderate alignment, several Russian narratives present
- **4**: Strong alignment with Russian messaging
- **5**: Nearly identical to Russian state propaganda

### ACTIVE_MEASURES_SIGNATURE (0-5)
Assesses structural similarity to historical active measures disinformation tradecraft:
- **0**: No active measures characteristics
- **1**: Minimal disinformation techniques
- **2**: Some manipulation techniques present
- **3**: Moderate use of active measures tactics
- **4**: Strong active measures signature
- **5**: Classic active measures operation characteristics

### USEFUL_IDIOT_PATTERN (0-5)
Evaluates how much the text unknowingly advances Russian strategic interests:
- **0**: No advancement of Russian interests
- **1**: Minimal inadvertent assistance
- **2**: Some unintentional promotion of Russian positions
- **3**: Moderate unconscious alignment
- **4**: Strong useful idiot pattern
- **5**: Significant unwitting advancement of Russian goals

## Usage Notes

- Patterns can co-occur and should be scored independently
- Confidence levels should be tracked for each pattern detection
- Context and source credibility should be considered in final scoring
- Regular updates needed as Russian propaganda evolves
